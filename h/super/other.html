Iyoshi Cola

Kola Kobayashi, representative of Iyoshi Cola, was born in Tokyo in 1989. He is the grandson of Ryotaro Ito, a craftsman who made traditional Japanese medicines. After graduating from the Hokkaido University School of Agriculture, and the Graduate School of Agricultural and Life Sciences, University of Tokyo, he began a journey exploring how to make cola, his most beloved drink, while employed at a major company. Finally, in July 2018, he launched Iyoshi Cola.

Kola Kobayashi has traveled throughout the world tasting cola, and he spent years attempting to create his own out of his desire to “create the ultimate cola that will bring joy to the person who drinks it.”

Located in Shimo-Ochiai, Tokyo, this cola factory was established by refurbishing the traditional Japanese medicine factory of Iyoshi Cola repesentive Kola Kobayashi’s grandfather, Ryotaro Ito.

The spice grinders and other tools used back then have been repaired and continue to be used today.

In West Africa, where the kola nut is grown, they have a saying, “Onye wetara oji, wetara ndu”, or “He who brings the kola nut, brings life.”

It is made by blending more than 10 spices and citrus fruits, including kola nuts, in secret proportions, and then fire-glazed in a method inspired by Kampo medicine. Iyoshi Cola initially served its freshly made craft cola in a mobile vending van, the “Kingfisher”.
